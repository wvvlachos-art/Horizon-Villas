About Horizon Villas

Six holiday houses around one shared pool, above Agios Ioannis on Tinos.

Welcome to Horizon Villas, a small collection of six holiday houses set around a shared pool on the hillside above Agios Ioannis, on the island of Tinos. Known for its peaceful setting, its views over the Aegean and its family-run hospitality, Horizon combines a modern Cycladic design — dry-stone walls and whitewash, timber-beamed ceilings and polished concrete, softened with woven and rattan touches — with the comfort of a fully equipped private house, an ideal base for couples and families exploring the quiet side of the Cyclades.

Six houses with sea views in Tinos

Each of the six houses — Zoe, Diana, Regina, Celine, Roxana and Astra — follows the same clean, contemporary design, in sizes for two to six guests. All come with a full kitchen, air-conditioning, fast wifi and outdoor dining space, and every stay includes daily cleaning, fresh linens, free parking and a welcome basket on arrival.

At the centre of the property, the shared pool and its sun terraces are the natural meeting point of the day, just a few steps from every door.

A quiet location above Agios Ioannis beach

Horizon Villas sits on a quiet hillside above Agios Ioannis, around five minutes on foot from the beach and its taverna. Tinos Town, with its port, restaurants and shops, is about fifteen minutes away by car, while the island's well-known villages — Pyrgos, Kardiani, Volax — make easy day trips.

The location combines easy access to everything the island has to offer with the privacy and calm of a small village setting.

Frequently asked questions

How many houses are there?

Six — Zoe, Diana, Regina, Celine, Roxana and Astra — all on one hillside around a single shared pool.

Do all the houses share the pool?

Yes. There's one pool, shared by all six houses, a few steps from each door.

What's included in a stay?

Daily cleaning, fresh linens, fast wifi, free parking and a welcome basket on arrival — in every house, every stay.

Is it suitable for families?

Very. Several houses sleep up to six, the pool is the centre of the day, and the beach is a five-minute walk down.

How do we get there?

Fly to Athens or Mykonos, then take the ferry to Tinos. We're about fifteen minutes from the port, above Agios Ioannis — and we'll help you plan the journey.

When is the best time to visit?

Spring (April to early June) and autumn (mid-September to October) are our favourites — warm, quiet, and the island at its best.

How far is the beach?

About five minutes on foot — a quiet swimming cove below the village, with a beach taverna for lunch.

Is there a minimum stay?

Usually a few nights, and it varies by season. Tell us your dates and we'll let you know what works.

Can we book directly?

Always — and it's the best price. Book direct and you deal with us directly, with no commission in between.
