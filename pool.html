The pool

The heart of it

One infinity pool, shared by all six houses, on a quiet hillside above the bay at Agios Ioannis.

One pool, six doors

We could have built six small pools. We built one good one instead — a village square with water in it, where the day begins and ends at Horizon.

Mornings

Coffee, and a quiet swim

The early hours belong to whoever is up first — a coffee on the deck, a lap or two in the still water before the heat, the bay waking up below the infinity edge.

Evenings

A drink as the sun drops

Later, the pool is for the grown-ups — a drink in hand on the daybeds as the sun drops behind the hills, the water turning gold and then to dusk. The one place all six houses share.

Shared, honestly

What "shared" actually means

How shared is shared?

Six houses share the pool — at full occupancy that's a handful of families, and in practice you'll rarely see more than a few people at the water at once. It has never felt like a hotel pool, and we intend to keep it that way.

What about children?

Afternoons are family hours; early mornings and evenings tend to be quiet. There's no lifeguard — children swim with their parents.

Do I need to claim a lounger at dawn?

No. There are loungers, parasols and shade for every house, and towels are provided — the 7 a.m. towel ritual is not a thing here.

When is the pool open?

Through the whole season, from early morning until late evening. We ask for quiet after midnight — sound carries on the hillside.
